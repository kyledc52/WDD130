Home page

Good foods for swimming include pasta, sandwiches, fruits and veggies, cereal, and yogurt. If you want that extra dose of energy before your swim, I recommend eating pasta, or anything with carbs. I know something that helped me during the swim season was eating a bagel before my workout. It made my experience with swimming so much better. When it comes to swimming, carbohydrates are your best friend. Don’t forget about chocolate milk! Milk has lots of nutrients. It is a great protein source. Chocolate milk has a great flavor. I’d recommend drinking it after your workout. You can, of course, supplement it with a protein drink, but that is up to you. Swimming is by far one of the best sports that will work most of your whole body. It even works the smaller muscles that support your joints. I say this from experience. After a workout, if I didn’t stretch, the parts that I worked the most will be aching. Like other sports, stretching is a must. If you don’t do it, you are just hurting yourself. I know shopping for swimwear and gear can be tricky. In my experience, I have had to gone through at least 50 pairs of goggles. Some places that are good for shopping for swimwear are amazon.com, swimoutlet.com, us.speedo.com, and arenasport.com. From my experience, there were some things I didn’t like about swimming. One of those is having to always put lotion on. Your skin will get dry. Another thing is having a runny nose all the time. You will constantly have to blow your nose. The last thing is being tired. This depends on how much sleep you get. If you are planning to swim the next day, please just make sure to get a good night’s rest. Strokes Page

When it comes to talking about swim strokes, they each have their own function of what muscles they work. The strokes that I will be addressing are Butterfly, Backstroke, Breaststroke, and Freestyle (the 4 main strokes). Butterfly This stroke works your core and lower back. The core and lower back lift your body out of the water upwards. The glutes control your legs; they keep them together. Your pectorals, lats, hamstrings, calves, shoulders, biceps, and triceps get worked hard when performing this stroke correctly. In my opinion, this stroke is painful, but when you get good at it, it can be fast. Breaststroke The middle and lower back muscles are used with every cycle of arm stroke you do (pectorals and latissimus). When kicking, the glutes and quads are the main muscles that do the work. Backstroke and Freestyle These 2 strokes are very similar. The only difference between them is that you are either laying on your front or back. With each cycle of stroke, your biceps, triceps, and back muscles are doing most of the work. When you want to breath, you have to use your neck muscles to turn your head. It is recommended when doing these 2 strokes that you have your hips high. This helps you float and glide through the water efficiently. It will also just make the stroke way less tiring (it will feel like you are doing less work). When kicking, the muscles that are used are the quads, hamstrings, and calves. Workouts Page

20 Minutes of Fun 4x Round 1 - 4x 50 Freestyle @1min, 1x 25 @1min Round 2 – 3x 50 Freestyle @1min, 2x 25 Freestyle @1min Round 3 – 2x 50 Freestyle @1min, 3x 25 Freestyle @1min Round 4 – 1x 50 Freestyle @1min, 4x 25 Freestyle @1min

U.S.R.P.T. (Ultra-Fast Race Pace Training) - You will need a clock to record your time 20x 50s @1min on goal time (200 pace) 20x 25s @30secs on goal time (100 pace) 10x 100s @2min on goal time (500 pace) If you miss your time, you sit out for one and go for the next one (50s and 100s), and for 25s, you sit out for 2. After completing work out, how many did you make? Record it and try it again later to see if you can beat your time.
